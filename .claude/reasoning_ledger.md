## Positions

### Breadth through depth (Aida)
**Hold:** Aida serves the broad neurodivergent spectrum by learning each individual deeply (per-person theory-of-mind), never by designing to a generic "average neurodivergent" persona. ToM is the core engine, not a feature — and it unifies emotional-translation (Problem 1) and memory-continuity (Problem 2) into one per-person model, where P2 is the memory face of the same engine.
**Because:** the average neurodivergent user is a fiction — alexithymia, autism, ADHD, TBI diverge hard. Depth per person is the only honest route to breadth. The two problems share one substrate (the per-person model), so ToM carries both.
**Drop it if:** a non-personalised baseline (one generic tone/subtext overlay, no per-person learning) turns out to defuse most real misreads on its own — then per-person ToM is gold-plating, and breadth-via-average wins on simplicity and cost.
**Formed:** 2026-06-12, when Dima corrected the framing to broad-spectrum + ToM-paramount. Expect this position to be tested at the Rams "cut everything" tier — that is the point.
