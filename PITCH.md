## 30-second table pitch (for when staff walk up)
> "Aida is captions for emotional subtext. I'm neurodivergent — I can't read the feeling under text, and I can't feel what my own messages carry. Aida reads incoming messages and tells me what people actually mean, grounded in a per-person emotional history it builds over time. And when I write something that would land as a wound, it locks the send until I fix it. It's all invented fresh on Opus 4.8 — the emotional model isn't borrowed, it's the product."

## Likely hard questions → answers
- **"Isn't this just sentiment analysis?"** → "Sentiment reads text in isolation. Aida reads each message against a *per-person* emotional history — the same words mean different things from different people. That per-person model is the whole thing, and it's built fresh, not an off-the-shelf taxonomy."
- **"Is this a mental-health / therapy tool?"** → "No — it's accessibility, like captions. It translates communication; it gives no advice, no diagnosis, no therapy."
- **"What did Opus 4.8 do that surprised you?"** → "It builds and layers a coherent emotional 'bank account' per person from raw text, and reasons about a message as a *deviation* from that person's baseline — theory-of-mind, not classification."
- **"What's the orchestration story?"** → "A frozen brief + a rubric the model grades itself against + a test set + a responding URL. Another team could rerun it tomorrow."
