# Tier 0 — The Person

> Start with the person who lives this. Everything flows from their actual experience, not from abstraction.

## Who this is for

**Aida is built for the broad neurodivergent spectrum** — people whose wiring makes emotional subtext and/or relational memory inaccessible: alexithymia, autism, ADHD, TBI, and others. The solve target is the *population*, not one user.

**Dima is the anchor, not the target.** We design from one real, articulate lived experience (his) so the work never drifts into a generic persona — but the product serves many. The reconciliation is the architecture itself: **breadth through depth.** Aida serves a wide population by learning each *individual* deeply (per-person **theory-of-mind**), never by designing to an "average neurodivergent user," which is a fiction — these conditions diverge hard.

**Theory-of-mind is paramount.** It is the core engine, and it is what unifies the two problems: the same per-person model holds *how this person communicates emotionally* (Problem 1) **and** *the history of the relationship with them* (Problem 2). Problem 2 is the memory face of the same engine, not a second feature.

## Who (the anchor)

**Dima** — the lived-experience anchor.

Two intersecting gaps that compound each other's harm:

### Gap 1: Emotional Blindness

**Cause:** Neurodivergence rooted in severe early-life trauma.

**What it means:** Cannot perceive emotions — neither in others nor in himself. The emotional layer is invisible in both directions.

**Inward (interoception):** He cannot perceive his own emotions. What he *can* feel is **somatic** — needles, warmth, a direction of flow. Raw bodily sensation. But the sensation does not map to a name: he cannot tell sad from happy, angry from depressed, from the feeling alone. He reports the somatic data; an AI currently helps translate it into an emotion. His words: *"it's bad."*

**The 2022 shift — this is not static:** Before 2022, he felt **nothing** — no somatic signal at all. The somatic layer (needles, warmth, flow) came online around 2022 and is *new*. He considers it lucky. **Implication: the capacity is emergent and possibly still developing. Aida is not compensating for a fixed deficit — it may be scaffolding a sense that is still coming in.**

**Outward:** The emotional content of others' communication is equally flat. Text arrives as literal meaning only.

**The cost:** When someone writes *"sure, that's fine"* most people feel instantly whether it's genuine or a wall going up. For Dima, it's flat. He reads it eight, ten times, running it forward as assent and backward as anger, and cannot settle it. Re-reading doesn't help — the decoder is broken, not just running slow.

#### Channels, ranked (best → worst readable)

1. **Facial expressions — GOOD.** When a person is physically present, the face gives real signal. His strongest channel.
2. **Body language — medium.** Partial signal.
3. **Text — flat.** Reads as *verbatim instructions*. No subtext exists for him; words are literal commands with nothing underneath.
4. **Tone of voice — WORST.** All speech is perceived as *screaming*. He cannot tell a gentle voice from a harsh one. Laughter is just "loud cackles" — he cannot tell whether someone is laughing *for* him, *at* him, or *with* him. Vocal tone carries no meaning.

> **Scoping signal:** his best channel (face) requires physical presence; his worst (tone) and a flat one (text) dominate remote/async communication. The damage likely concentrates where the face is absent.

#### Intensity — binary, not graded

Emotions register as **On/Off**. He has no native sense of an emotion's *temperature* (intensity/gradient) — he believed feelings simply exist or don't, full stop. That emotions have *degrees* was **taught to him externally** — learned, recent, not native perception.

#### Timing — post-hoc, hours-long

He does not perceive emotion in the moment. He realizes *after the fact* that someone got upset, then sits with himself **reliving the moments for hours**, reasoning his way to *why*. Understanding arrives by slow deduction, never by feeling.

#### The somatic language — the inward channel he DOES have

What he feels instead of emotion is a structured bodily phenomenology — high-resolution, precisely describable:

- **Medium:** water, wind, needles — *("flooding water in my lungs, like I can't breathe")*
- **Temperature:** hot or cold — cold wind, cold water, hot wind, hot water
- **Motion:** directional flow · wave (snake-like) · pulse · static ("just sits") · radial spread · expansion beyond the body · reaching/directed toward a point
- **Direction:** mostly internal; some reach *outward-forward*; some go *upward* (toward the sky)
- **Shape:** the wave can carry a distinct shape

He can describe all of this with precision. What he **cannot** do is map any of it to an emotion name. **Rich somatic input, absent emotional label — that is the precise center of Problem 1.**

#### The compounded cost — unread intent defaults to threat

In-person is only *slightly* easier; presence does not lift the gap. The failure runs **both ways**:

- **He is misread:** his directness reads as *rudeness* to others.
- **He misreads others — toward danger:** unknown intent does not stay neutral. He cannot tell an evil smile from a genuine one, words of concern from words of contempt. The unreadable collapses into *assumed malice*, putting him in a **paranoid state**.

The sharpest edge of Problem 1 is **not** "he feels nothing about others." It is that **the absence of readable intent fills with worst-case intent.** Unread emotion defaults to threat. This is the line the whole product turns on: Aida's first job is to replace a guess that defaults to danger with a grounded read.

### Gap 2: Traumatic Brain Injury

**Cause:** A traumatic brain injury (TBI).

**What it means:** Terminal memory loss — conversation memory beyond 3 hours is gone. The thread of a relationship doesn't persist on its own. What was the emotional state we left it in two days ago? Unknown. Was that person angry, warm, mid-disagreement? Lost.

Long-term memory survives only as snapshots — crystallized moments. Current-day working memory: last 3 hours maximum.

**The cost:** Without relational continuity, every conversation resets. Emotional subtext requires knowing the person's baseline, the arc you're in, what happened before. With a 3-hour window and no emotional perception, everything is starting cold.

---

## How These Gaps Interact

**Alone**, each is survivable. Together, they create a collapse:

- Can't read subtext → need memory to remember the person's normal emotional baseline
- Can't remember beyond 3 hours → need someone else to hold continuity
- Can't perceive emotions → need external translation of what's underneath the words

Aida exists at this intersection. It's not solving the TBI or the neurodivergence. It's building a bridge that lets him have continuity and understanding anyway.

---

## The Lived Moment

A message comes in. Dima sees literal text. He has no sense of the sender's emotional state — is this person okay? Angry? Afraid? The words alone don't tell him.

He also doesn't remember: What was the arc with this person? What's their baseline? When did we last talk? What happened then?

He re-reads. Once, twice, ten times. The emotional truth doesn't emerge. The previous conversation is gone. He's trapped in a loop of literal interpretation with no continuity and no emotional ground.

**This is the problem Aida addresses.**

---

## Why This Matters

Dima has relationships, partnerships, work. He reads, thinks, loves. The gaps don't touch any of that.

But they specifically, surgically disable:
- Reading subtext in human communication
- Remembering human relationships across time

These are the two things required to know another person.

Aida's job: provide what the injury and neurodivergence removed — continuity and emotional translation — so he can have genuine relationships anyway.
